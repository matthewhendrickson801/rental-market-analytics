## Executive Summary

This report focuses exclusively on Duval County ZIP codes within Jacksonville city limits, excluding St. Augustine and outlying areas (St. Johns County). Our analysis covers **51 core Jacksonville ZIP codes** to provide StateofJax with actionable insights for community development within Duval County.

### Key Findings

**Market Overview:**
- **51 Duval County ZIP codes** analyzed
- **Average rent:** $1,485 (vs. $1,450 predicted)
- **Market efficiency:** 97.6% (rents only 2.4% above predicted)
- **Median income range:** $30,514 to $150,736

**Affordability Opportunities:**
- **2 highly affordable ZIPs** (rent 15%+ below predicted)
- **7 affordable ZIPs** (rent 5-15% below predicted)
- **28 fair value ZIPs** (within ±5% of predicted)
- **14 overpriced ZIPs** (rent 5%+ above predicted)

**Bottom Line:** Duval County has a remarkably efficient rental market with limited arbitrage opportunities. Focus should be on promoting suburban value neighborhoods and addressing overpricing in specific pockets.

## Duval County Market Analysis

### Overall Market Efficiency: 97.6%

Duval County's rental market is highly efficient, with actual rents averaging only 2.4% above predicted values. This indicates:
- **Transparent pricing:** Rents closely track economic fundamentals
- **Limited speculation:** Few overpriced or underpriced areas
- **Fair market:** Renters generally pay appropriate rates for neighborhood quality

## Conclusion

Duval County Jacksonville has a remarkably efficient rental market (97.6%) with limited arbitrage opportunities. The focus for StateofJax should be:

1. **Promote suburban value** in 32068, 32217, 32211, 32207, 32223
2. **Address overpricing** in 32254, 32063, 32145, 32040
3. **Invest in urban core** economic development (32202, 32206, 32208, 32209)
4. **Monitor growth areas** for affordability erosion
5. **Celebrate efficiency** as competitive advantage

The data shows Jacksonville offers genuine affordability compared to peer cities, with fair pricing across most neighborhoods. Strategic interventions in specific pockets can maximize housing accessibility for Duval County residents.
