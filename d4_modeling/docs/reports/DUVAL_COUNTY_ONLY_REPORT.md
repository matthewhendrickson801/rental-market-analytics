## Executive Summary

This report focuses exclusively on the 31 Duval County ZIP codes within Jacksonville city limits that have complete data in our analysis. We exclude all outlying counties (Clay, Nassau, St. Johns, Bradford) to provide StateofJax with actionable insights specifically for Duval County.

### Key Findings

**Market Overview:**
- **31 Duval County ZIP codes** analyzed (core Jacksonville only)
- **Average rent:** $1,476 (vs. $1,442 predicted)
- **Market efficiency:** 97.6% (rents only 2.4% above predicted)
- **Median income range:** $30,514 to $150,736

**Affordability Distribution:**
- **0 highly affordable ZIPs** (rent 15%+ below predicted) in core Duval
- **6 affordable ZIPs** (rent 5-15% below predicted)
- **21 fair value ZIPs** (within ±5% of predicted)
- **4 overpriced ZIPs** (rent 5%+ above predicted)

**Bottom Line:** Core Duval County has an exceptionally efficient rental market (97.6%) with most neighborhoods priced fairly. Limited arbitrage opportunities exist, but several suburban areas offer 5-15% affordability advantages.

## Conclusion

Core Duval County Jacksonville has an exceptionally efficient rental market (97.6%) with 67.7% of ZIP codes showing fair-value rents. The focus for StateofJax should be:

1. **Promote suburban value** in 32217 (Mandarin West), 32211 (Normandy), 32207 (San Marco)
2. **Address rent burden** in 32254 (Westside), 32202 (Downtown)
3. **Invest in urban core** economic development (32202, 32206, 32209)
4. **Monitor growth areas** (32219, 32233, 32220) for affordability erosion
5. **Celebrate efficiency** as competitive advantage

The data shows core Duval County offers genuine affordability in desirable neighborhoods (Mandarin, San Marco, Riverside) while maintaining fair pricing across most of the market. Strategic interventions in specific pockets (32254, 32202) can maximize housing accessibility for Duval County residents.
