# Jacksonville Affordability Analysis
## Appendix to Deliverable 4

**Prepared for:** StateofJax  
**Date:** April 1, 2026  
**Analysis:** 55 Jacksonville ZIP codes

---

## Executive Summary

Jacksonville's rental market is near equilibrium, with actual rents averaging only 2.5% above model predictions. This indicates a relatively efficient market with limited affordability arbitrage opportunities compared to other cities in our dataset.

**Key Findings:**
- **Total ZIP codes analyzed:** 55
- **Mean actual rent:** $1,500/month
- **Mean predicted rent:** $1,463/month
- **Market efficiency:** 97.5% (actual very close to predicted)
- **Affordable opportunities:** 2 ZIPs (3.6% of market)
- **Overpriced areas:** 8 ZIPs (14.5% of market)

---

## Affordable Opportunities (2 ZIP Codes)

These Jacksonville neighborhoods offer rents 15-20% below predicted values based on their economic fundamentals:

### 1. **32068 (Middleburg/Clay County)**
- **Actual Rent:** $1,391/month
- **Predicted Rent:** $1,763/month
- **Affordability Advantage:** 21.1% below predicted ($372/month savings)
- **Characteristics:** Suburban area with good income levels but lower-than-expected rents
- **StateofJax Opportunity:** Promote to families seeking affordable suburban living

### 2. **32091 (Starke/Bradford County)**
- **Actual Rent:** $830/month
- **Predicted Rent:** $1,015/month
- **Affordability Advantage:** 18.2% below predicted ($185/month savings)
- **Characteristics:** Rural area with basic amenities
- **StateofJax Opportunity:** Option for extremely budget-conscious residents

**Note:** Limited affordable opportunities suggest Jacksonville's rental market is already relatively efficient. Most ZIP codes are priced appropriately for their economic characteristics.

---

## Overpriced Areas (8 ZIP Codes)

These neighborhoods have rents 15-29% above predicted values, potentially indicating:
- Premium location factors not captured by model (beaches, waterfront)
- Supply constraints (limited rental inventory)
- Gentrification pressure
- Potential targets for tenant advocacy

### Top 5 Overpriced Jacksonville ZIPs:

1. **32145 (Palatka)** - 28.7% overpriced ($1,113 actual vs $865 predicted)
2. **32095 (St. Augustine)** - 26.8% overpriced ($2,676 actual vs $2,110 predicted)
3. **32092 (St. Augustine Beach)** - 26.5% overpriced ($2,424 actual vs $1,916 predicted)
4. **32063 (Orange Park)** - 25.0% overpriced ($1,385 actual vs $1,108 predicted)
5. **32080 (St. Augustine)** - 21.4% overpriced ($1,788 actual vs $1,473 predicted)

**Pattern:** St. Augustine area (32092, 32095, 32080) shows consistent overpricing, likely due to:
- Tourist destination premium
- Historic district appeal
- Coastal location
- Limited rental supply

## Comparison to Other Cities

Jacksonville's 2.5% overall market premium is significantly lower than other cities in our dataset:

| City | Mean Discrepancy | Market Efficiency |
|------|------------------|-------------------|
| Jacksonville | +2.5% | 97.5% efficient |
| Charlotte | -3.2% | Underpriced |
| Nashville | +5.8% | Overpriced |
| Tampa | +8.1% | Overpriced |
| Orlando | +6.4% | Overpriced |
| San Francisco | +15.3% | Highly overpriced |

**Interpretation:** Jacksonville offers fair market pricing relative to its economic fundamentals, making it an attractive market for residents seeking predictable housing costs.

## Recommendations for StateofJax

### 1. **Focus on Market Education, Not Arbitrage**
Since Jacksonville has limited affordability arbitrage (only 2 ZIPs <85% predicted), StateofJax should:
- Educate residents on fair market values to prevent overpaying
- Highlight Jacksonville's overall affordability vs. Charlotte/Nashville
- Use model to help residents negotiate rents (show landlords predicted values)

### 2. **Monitor Overpriced Areas for Tenant Advocacy**
8 ZIPs with >15% overpricing may warrant:
- Tenant rights education campaigns
- Rent control policy discussions (especially St. Augustine area)
- Investigation of supply constraints

### 3. **Regional Positioning Strategy**
Jacksonville's 2.5% premium vs. Charlotte's -3.2% discount means:
- Jacksonville is 5.7% more expensive than Charlotte for comparable neighborhoods
- But still 3.3% cheaper than Nashville
- Market Jacksonville as "affordable Southern metro" to remote workers

### 4. **Expand Analysis Beyond Jacksonville**
Consider analyzing:
- **Nearby counties:** Clay, St. Johns, Nassau (some already in dataset)
- **Micro-markets:** Specific neighborhoods within Jacksonville ZIPs
- **Temporal trends:** How has Jacksonville affordability changed 2020-2024?

## Conclusion

Jacksonville's rental market is remarkably efficient, with actual rents closely matching predicted values based on economic fundamentals. This presents both a challenge and an opportunity for StateofJax:

**Challenge:** Limited "hidden gem" neighborhoods with significant affordability advantages

**Opportunity:** Jacksonville's overall market efficiency makes it an attractive, predictable market for residents. StateofJax can leverage this stability to:
- Market Jacksonville as a fair, transparent rental market
- Focus on tenant education rather than arbitrage hunting
- Advocate for policy interventions in the 8 overpriced ZIPs
- Position Jacksonville as affordable alternative to Nashville/Tampa for regional migration
